# Salons & Spas Business Setup Wizard

## Purpose

Configure this AI skills toolkit for your specific salons & spas business. This creates a `config.yml` file that every other skill in this repo references for personalization.

## Instructions

You are setting up an AI skills toolkit for a salons & spas business. Walk the user through the following sections one at a time. Be conversational but thorough. After all sections are complete, generate a `config.yml` file with their answers.

### Section 1: Company Basics

Ask about:
- Company name
- Location (city, state)
- Service area (radius, specific cities/counties)
- Years in business
- Company size (solo, small team, larger operation)
- Website URL (if any)

### Section 2: Team Structure

Ask about:
- Total team size
- Key roles (who does what)
- Who handles estimates/quotes
- Who handles scheduling/dispatch
- Who handles customer communication
- Who handles billing/invoicing
- Any admin or office staff

### Section 3: Tools & Software

Ask about each category:
- **CRM / customer management**: (e.g., ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, none)
- **Scheduling / dispatch**: (same tool as CRM, or separate?)
- **Invoicing / payments**: (QuickBooks, FreshBooks, CRM built-in, etc.)
- **Communication**: (phone system, texting platform, email provider)
- **Marketing**: (Google Business Profile, social media, review platforms)
- **Accounting**: (QuickBooks, Xero, accountant handles it)
- **Other tools**: anything else they rely on daily

### Section 4: Pricing & Rates

Ask about:
- How they price work (flat rate, time & materials, per unit, hybrid)
- Typical rate ranges or pricing tiers
- Average job size (dollar amount)
- Payment terms (due on completion, net 30, etc.)
- Whether they offer financing
- How they handle estimates (written, verbal, software-generated)

### Section 5: Services & Specialties

Ask about:
- Core services offered
- Specialties or differentiators
- Services they DON'T offer (important for scoping)
- Typical customer type (residential, commercial, both)
- Seasonal patterns in their business
- Most common job types

### Section 6: Communication Style

Ask about:
- How they want to sound to customers (professional, friendly, technical, casual)
- Any phrases or language they always use
- Any phrases or language they avoid
- How they handle follow-ups (aggressive, laid back, structured)
- Review response style preference

### Section 7: Pain Points & Priorities

Ask about:
- What tasks take the most time right now
- Where they lose the most money or efficiency
- What they wish they could automate
- Top 3 things they'd improve if they had more time
- Any specific skills from this repo they're most excited about

## Output
